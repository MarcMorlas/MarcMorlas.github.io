Synopsis

This project is the final assignment for the UX/UI course. In this case, we decided to create the game for the Quest2, as it presented a significant challenge that none of the team members had faced before.

The game aims to convey to the player is stress, although the mechanics are simple. The player takes on the role of a novice teacher in a difficult class, where they must correct exams while students constantly interrupt, making the task harder and increasing the stress level. Through these mechanics, the game creates a real sense of stress.



Development


Exam Mechanics/Correcting Mechanics

The main mechanic of the game is the grading of exams and how it works. There were several iterations regarding how the user could interact with the exams to grade them. Essentially, the user needs to look at the exam they wish to grade and count how many correct answers the student has given. On their desk, they will have five cubes, each with a number on them.

To grade an exam, the user must place their hand over a cube, press the controller's "Trigger" to grab and activate it. At that moment, the cube will turn yellow to indicate that it is active, and when the cube touches any exam, it will automatically grade it. The exams are chosen randomly from a pool of pre-created exams. Below are the iterations that occurred during development.

In the first iteration, once the player had grabbed the cube, they had to press one of the controller's triggers again to activate it while holding it. This iteration seemed more "realistic," but during testing with several users, the mechanic was not fully understood and was not as precise as we had thought.

In the second iteration, which we chose to keep, the "grading cube" remains activated as long as the user is holding it.



Tutorial

I was responsible for the complete development of the tutorial. The tutorial was a significant challenge since it was a VR game; we wanted everyone to know how to play, so we had to be as clear as possible.

One of the most difficult aspects of our project was making people understand that to punish the students, you literally had to aim at the student and shout at them.
